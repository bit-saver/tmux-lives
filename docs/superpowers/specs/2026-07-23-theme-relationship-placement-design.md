# Theme v4 — relationship, placement, and mode

Status: approved in brainstorm 2026-07-23, not yet planned or built. Supersedes the v3.2/v3.3 palette derivation (`barpos` / `kincap` / `kintabs` / accent ring / `--rotate`).

## The problem

Live use of v3.2/v3.3 produced three complaints: the bar and endcap read as too similar, `--rotate` does nothing of consequence, and the bar/cap/tabs "trio" doesn't earn its keep aesthetically.

Measurement confirmed all three are structural rather than tuning misses. Across all ten schemes at the live seed, bar→cap was ΔL 0.099–0.101 and bar→tabs was ΔL 0.159–0.161 — the ±0.10 (`kincap`) and ±0.16 (`kintabs`) steps are hardcoded, so **every scheme produces the identical pair relationship** and differs only in the hue it is anchored at. Rotate leaves `bar`, `tabs`, `cap` and `text` byte-identical across all five states, permuting only three accent colors; at `contrast=darker` on a mid-dark bar those three land darker than the bar and are close to invisible.

The v3.2 `kincap` rule was fitted from a calibration study that judged **large isolated bar+endcap tiles**. The real endcap is a small slanted sliver and the tab strip is a separate band stacked on the bar. The study optimised harmony at a size and adjacency the actual UI does not have.

## Evidence this design is built on

Two blind, multi-select mockup rounds, rendering the tab strip stacked on the status bar with the endcap at true size.

**Round 1** (six separation rules, one seed `#5f772b`) — accepted 1, 5, 6; rejected 2, 3, 4.

- Every accepted option keeps the **tab strip quiet** (near the bar in lightness and chroma). Every rejected option had a loud tab strip — bright (`#bbe4a4`), saturated, or far-hued. This directly contradicts v3.3 `kintabs`, which pushes tabs *further* from the bar (ΔL 0.16) than the cap (ΔL 0.10).
- The separation lever that works is **chroma contrast** — a muted field with a saturated accent — not a taller lightness ladder (rejected as #2) and not hue distance (#5 same-hue and #6 at 60° were both accepted).
- Pure hue separation at flat lightness (#3) and wide hue-plus-depth (#4) were both rejected.

**Round 1 follow-up:** the user identified a confound — every candidate shared one seed, so "more colourful" could only mean "more olive", and the two multi-hue candidates were generated by mechanical hue rotation at fixed lightness. This matches the prior palette research: geometric hue-harmony appears in zero shipped design systems, and IBM Carbon abandoned computational generation with this same symptom. A good palette is one curve, where hue travels *with* lightness.

**Round 2** (nine palettes, nine different anchors, hue travelling along a rising lightness ramp) — **all nine accepted.** Travel magnitudes of 0°, 35°, 55°, 70° and 85° all landed, across olive, teal, blue, purple, amber, rust and green anchors.

The model below is round 2's structure, made configurable.

## The model

A theme is a curve. Four things select it: **relationship**, **placement**, **mode**, and the existing fine knobs (phase, vividness, shape, ease, contrast).

### The curve

Three surface roles sit at fixed positions. These values are round 2's, and they encode round 1's finding — muted field, quiet tabs, saturated accent.

| role | position `t` | lightness target | chroma target |
|---|---|---|---|
| bar | 0.00 | 0.40 | 0.045 |
| tabs | 0.42 | 0.51 | 0.062 |
| endcap | 1.00 | 0.66 | 0.115 |

Hue is `H(t) = anchorHue + drift · t`. Text and accent colors remain computed for contrast against the actual resulting bar, as in v3.2.

### Relationship

`drift` — total hue travel from bar to endcap. This is the new top-level axis and the thing round 2 tested directly.

Proposed set, **subject to calibration**: flat 0°, drift 35°, lean 55°, span 75°, reach 110°. Round 2 provides evidence for 0–85°; the 110° value is extrapolation and is the first thing calibration should confirm or move.

Travel direction (warm-ward vs cool-ward) is a separate degree of freedom from magnitude — round 2 accepted both, at `+40`/`+55` and at `−55`/`−70`/`−85`. Where it lives is unresolved; see Open items.

### Placement

Where the seed sits on the curve: `bar`, `tabs`, `cap`, plus two floating positions `low` (t ≈ 0.25) and `high` (t ≈ 0.75). The text and accent roles are too small to be worth placing on.

Placement necessarily bends the ramp: anchoring a dark seed at the endcap pulls the whole curve darker. This is intended — it is what generates genuinely new colors rather than a permutation of the same ones — but it is the mechanic most likely to produce a dud at an extreme seed, and calibration should watch for that.

### Mode

**Literal** — the placed role renders the seed's exact hex. The rest of the ramp is refitted so that `H(t_place) = seedH`, `L(t_place) = seedL` and `C(t_place) = seedC`, with the other roles keeping their relative spacing, clamped to stay monotonic and within `[0.05, 0.95]`.

**Derived** — the seed is the basis only and its hex is not rendered anywhere.

### How the seed stays foundational in derived mode

The requirement is that the seed governs every scheme, literal or not. In derived mode it does so three ways:

- **Hue** anchors the curve.
- **Lightness** shifts the whole ramp, *damped and clamped*: `offset = clamp(0.5 · (seedL − 0.51), −0.10, +0.10)`, applied to every role target. A dark seed reads as a deeper theme and a light seed as an airier one, so swapping seeds is always visibly different — but the bar's lightness stays inside roughly `[0.30, 0.50]` and can never be pushed bright.
- **Chroma** scales the arc ceiling against a reference of 0.078, damped and clamped by the same principle.

Damping is the specific fix for a tension this project has already hit from both sides. Full seed lightness (v3.1, current) puts the seed's L unfiltered onto the largest surface, which is why a bright seed yields a bright bar — the failure round 1 identified. Zero seed lightness (v3, before it) produced "seed changes did nothing" when two same-hue seeds of different brightness gave identical output, and was explicitly rejected. Damping keeps seed changes visible without letting them wreck the ramp.

## The catalog

Each relationship owns **8 rows**:

| rows | what |
|---|---|
| 3 literal | seed at bar, tabs, cap — rendered as the exact hex |
| 3 derived | the same three anchor positions, seed as basis only |
| 2 floating | seed hue anchored between roles (`low` t≈0.25, `high` t≈0.75), always derived |

**Coverage guarantee:** every placeable role has at least one scheme in which the seed renders literally at that role — structurally, in every relationship. Schemes containing no literal seed are first-class, not a fallback: a basis color that is only the stepping-off point produces good palettes and generates colors the current engine cannot reach.

Five relationships × 8 rows = 40 palettes, browsed 8 at a time, versus 10 undifferentiated schemes today.

## Picker

Approach A: relationship is a knob, placements and modes are the list.

- The adjustments zone gains a **relationship** field and loses **rotate**.
- The scheme list shows the current relationship's 8 rows plus the `off` row and the anchor row — 10 rows, close to today's count, so the 52×27 frame, anchor row, preview and tab chip survive unchanged.
- Literal rows are marked with **✦**, which already means "the seed's home base" in the status bar, so the marker teaches itself.

```
✦ bar      seed renders as the status bar
✦ tabs     seed renders as the tab strip
✦ cap      seed renders as the endcap
  bar      derived, anchored at bar position
  tabs     derived
  cap      derived
  low      floating, seed hue at t≈0.25
  high     floating, seed hue at t≈0.75
```

- Relationship cycles on **`l`**, shift-reverses like the other cycling knobs. `o` is freed by retiring rotate but is deliberately left unbound rather than reused for an unrelated meaning.

## CLI

Relationship is the positional, since it is the top axis; placement and mode are flags.

```fish
tmux-lives setup theme span --place cap --mode literal
tmux-lives setup theme list              # the relationships
tmux-lives setup theme list span         # that relationship's 8 rows
```

`--place` takes `bar|tabs|cap|low|high`; `low` and `high` imply `--mode derived`. `--rotate` is removed and errors with a pointer to `--place` rather than being silently accepted. `--phase`, `--vividness`, `--shape`, `--ease` and `--contrast` are unchanged.

## Migration

Previously selected schemes are discarded outright; no mapping from the old ten names is attempted, because they encode arc widths rather than relationships and any mapping would land people somewhere arbitrary.

- **The seed is preserved** — `tmux_lives_bar_color` carries over untouched and is fully usable by the new system.
- `tmux_lives_theme` and `tmux_lives_theme_rotate` are erased.
- `phase`, `vividness`, `shape`, `ease`, `contrast` carry over.
- New universals for relationship, placement and mode.
- On `fisher update` the theme is set to the **first generated scheme for the existing seed** — catalog order puts relationship #1 (`flat`) with `--place bar --mode literal` first, which is closest to the current look. One-line notice, idempotent, in the same shape as the v2→v3 and v3.1 shims.

## Testing

Beyond pure-builder coverage of the sampler, relationship table, placement fitting and clamp maths:

- **Coverage invariant** — for every relationship, the 8 rows contain exactly 3 literal rows, one per placeable role. The guarantee above becomes a failing test if anyone breaks it.
- **Clamp invariant** — across extreme seeds (near-white, near-black, near-grey, maximum chroma) the ramp stays monotonic and the bar's lightness stays inside its band.
- **Migration** — idempotent; seed preserved; scheme and rotate universals erased; lands on the first catalog entry.
- **Grep guard** — no `rotate` references outside the migration shim.
- Picker geometry pins, in the existing style.

## Sequencing

1. **Calibration round** — fit the relationship magnitudes and row constants using the method that already worked here: blind numbered tiles, batches of ≤12, decoys in validation rounds. Only 0–85° of travel has evidence; the rest are estimates and should be fitted rather than asserted.
2. **Engine** — pure builders, testable without any UI, inspectable via `setup theme list`.
3. **CLI** — flags, help, migration.
4. **Picker** — the 8-row list, relationship knob, ✦ markers.

Engine before picker, so real output can be reviewed before the UI is committed to.

## Open items

- Relationship magnitudes and row constants are unfitted estimates pending calibration.
- **Travel direction** has no home yet. Options: a sixth/seventh row per relationship (doubling the list to 16 — likely too long), a separate cycling knob alongside relationship, or folding it into the sign of `--phase`. Worth settling during calibration, when we can see whether direction changes the character enough to deserve a control.
- The `reach` 110° value is extrapolation beyond any tested travel.
- Whether the migration should land on `flat/bar/literal` (closest to current) or on something more colorful that showcases the new range.
- Placement at extreme seeds is the most likely source of a dud palette and needs explicit calibration attention.
